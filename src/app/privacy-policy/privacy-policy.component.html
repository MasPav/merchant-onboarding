IT Consortium Data Privacy Consent

Introduction

IT Consortium, as a business entity operating in Ghana, is committed to protecting your personal data in compliance with the Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843). We collect and process your personal information for due diligence, Merchant KYC (Know Your Customer), identity verification, risk assessment, compliance, and to fulfill contractual obligations.

1. Purpose of Data Collection

We collect and process personal data for the following purposes:

  • Conducting due diligence and identity verification
  • Risk assessment and fraud prevention
  • Compliance with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Fulfilling contractual obligations

2. Types of Data Collected

We may collect the following types of personal data:

  • Full name
  • Date of birth
  • Address
  • Contact information (phone number, email)
  • Financial information
  • Identification documents (e.g., passport, national ID)

3. How We Process Your Data

Your personal data may be processed in the following ways:

  • Data entry and secure storage
  • Identity and risk verification
  • Analysis and reporting
  • Disclosure to third parties as necessary

4. Data Security

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to safeguard your personal data from unauthorized access, loss, or misuse, including encryption, access controls, and secure storage systems.

5. Data Retention

We retain personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, in accordance with legal and regulatory requirements.

6. Your Rights as a Data Subject

Under the Data Protection Act 2012 (Act 843), you have the right to:

  • Access: Request a copy of your personal data.
  • Rectification: Request corrections to inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Erasure: Request deletion of your personal data under certain conditions.
  • Restriction of Processing: Request limitations on how we process your data.
  • Data Portability: Request the transfer of your data to another organization.
  • Object to Processing: Object to certain types of data processing.

7. Withdrawal of Consent

You may withdraw your consent for us to process your personal data at any time. However, withdrawal may affect your ability to engage in business with us where data processing is necessary.
